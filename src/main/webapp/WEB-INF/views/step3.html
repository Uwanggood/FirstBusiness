Step 3: Please Identify Your Decision Factors

Help

When making basic decisions, you subconsciously identify factors relevant to the decision. These factors "weigh" and create the evaluative environment for decision making. For example, if you were evaluating a move from Chicago to Phoenixâ€”crime, family, schools, entertainment, cost of living, and job market might be relevent decision factors.

If the decision factor "weather" was extremely important to you, you would weigh that factor a 10. If your children are near 18 years old, the decision factor "schools" might hold less importance and weigh a 3.

Please identify ALL the factors relevant to this decision and WEIGHT them 1-10

Examples of decision factors:
Crime, cost of living, schools, weather, job market.
